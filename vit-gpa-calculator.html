VIT Grading Scale & Mapping

At Vellore Institute of Technology (VIT), each course is awarded a final letter grade based on overall academic performance. Performance is evaluated through a combination of Continuous Assessment and the Final Assessment Test (FAT).

Each letter grade is mapped to a numerical grade point under the official 10-point Credit-Based Grading System (CBGS). These grade points are the core components used to calculate both SGPA and CGPA.

Letter Grade Performance Description Grade Point
S Outstanding 10
A Excellent 9
B Very Good 8
C Good 7
D Fair 6
E Poor 5
F Fail / Backlog 0
N No Grade (Incomplete) 0

How the Grading System Works

  • Credit Weightage: Each subject’s grade point is multiplied by its credit value.
  • Impact Factor: Courses with higher credits have a significantly greater impact on your SGPA.
  • The "F" Grade: An "F" grade carries 0 grade points and mathematically reduces both your SGPA and CGPA totals.

How GPA is Calculated in VIT

Vellore Institute of Technology (VIT) utilizes a 10-point Credit-Based Grading System (CBGS). Under this system, your GPA is a credit-weighted average, meaning that a 4-credit theory course has a much larger impact on your final score than a 1-credit lab.

To calculate your GPA, three factors are considered:

  • Credit Value: The weightage assigned to the course.
  • Letter Grade: Your performance rank (S, A, B, etc.).
  • Grade Point: The numerical value of your letter grade (e.g., S = 10).

Official VIT SGPA Formula (Semester GPA)

The SGPA is calculated using the following credit-weighted formula:

SGPA = Σ (Credits × Grade Points) / Σ Total Semester Credits

Official VIT CGPA Formula (Cumulative GPA)

Your CGPA is the aggregate of all your semester performances:

CGPA = Σ Total Credit Points (All Semesters) / Σ Total Credits Registered

VIT CGPA to Percentage Conversion

According to official VIT Academic Regulations, the standard formula to convert your CGPA into a percentage is:

Percentage (%) = CGPA × 10

Example: A CGPA of 8.89 is equivalent to 88.9%.

Steps to Calculate Your GPA Manually

  1. Convert each letter grade into its corresponding grade point (S=10, A=9, etc.).
  2. Multiply the grade points by the course credits for each subject.
  3. Sum all the resulting credit points.
  4. Divide the total credit points by the total semester credits.

Note: Results are typically rounded to two decimal places as per standard university practice.

Grade Replacement & Improvement Policy

If you repeat a course to clear a backlog or improve your score, the GPA calculation follows specific Grade Replacement rules. We recommend verifying the latest policy on your VTOP Portal or academic handbook, as rules can vary by batch.
