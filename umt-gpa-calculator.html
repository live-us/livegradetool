UMT Grading System

The University of Management and Technology (UMT) uses a 4.00 GPA grading scale to evaluate student performance. Each letter grade corresponds to a specific grade point value that is used to calculate your semester GPA. Our UMT GPA calculator on this page uses the same grading system to ensure accurate and reliable results.

Here is the UMT grading scale:

These grade points are multiplied by the credit hours of each course to calculate total grade points. How UMT GPA Is Calculated

Calculating your GPA at UMT is simple once you know the formula. Your GPA is determined by dividing the total grade points earned by the total credit hours of all courses in a semester.

GPA Formula:

GPA = Total Grade Points ÷ Total Credit Hours

Step-by-Step Example:

  1. Suppose you have three courses:
    1. Course 1: A (4.0) × 3 credit hours = 12 grade points
    2. Course 2: B+ (3.3) × 3 credit hours = 9.9 grade points
    3. Course 3: B (3.0) × 4 credit hours = 12 grade points
  2. Add the total grade points: 12 + 9.9 + 12 = 33.9
  3. Add the total credit hours: 3 + 3 + 4 = 10
  4. Divide total grade points by total credit hours: 33.9 ÷ 10 = 3.39 GPA

This is exactly how the UMT GPA calculator on this page works—it multiplies each course’s grade point by its credit hours, sums the results, and divides by the total credits to give you an instant, accurate GPA.

What is the highest GPA a student can achieve at UMT?

The highest possible GPA at the University of Management and Technology (UMT) is 4.00. This represents excellent academic performance in a semester and follows the official UMT grading scale.

A CGPA of 2.8 usually corresponds to a B grade at UMT. Keep in mind that this is an average across all courses taken.

What is the UMT grading percentage scale?

The UMT grading percentage scale converts numeric marks to letter grades and GPA as follows: A (85–100%), A- (80–84%), B+ (75–79%), B (70–74%), B- (65–69%), C+ (60–64%), C (55–59%), C- (50–54%), D+ (45–49%), D (40–44%), F (<40%).

How do I convert GPA to CGPA at UMT?

To convert semester GPA to CGPA, sum the GPA of all completed semesters multiplied by their credit hours, then divide by the total credit hours completed. This gives your cumulative GPA on the 4.0 scale.
