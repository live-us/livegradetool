Frequently Asked Questions

What is LSAC GPA?

LSAC GPA is a standardized GPA calculated by the Law School Admission Council to compare applicants fairly across different institutions.

How is it different from my university GPA?

It includes all undergraduate coursework, applies a standardized grading scale, and may count repeated courses differently.

Does LSAC include repeated or failed courses?

Yes. LSAC typically includes all attempts, which can impact your final GPA.

Is this calculator accurate?

Yes. It follows LSAC grading rules and provides a reliable estimate of your official LSAC GPA.
